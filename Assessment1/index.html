Interactive Web Experience Review

Case Study: Gehry Getty Interactive

1. First Impression

The first thing I noticed was the large-scale architectural visuals and smooth scrolling transitions. The bold imagery immediately created a sense of immersion and suggested a visually driven experience.

2. User Actions (2 Minutes)

I scrolled through the page, hovered over elements, clicked into sections, explored media, paused to read, and revisited areas to better understand the layout.

3. Main Engagement

I spent the most time engaging with the scroll-based storytelling sections where visuals and text explained architectural ideas.

4. Most Common Action

The most common action was scrolling, as the experience is driven by vertical navigation.

5. Primary Goal

The experience aims to educate users about Frank Gehry’s architectural process while showcasing his work interactively.

6. Communication of Goal

This is communicated through structured storytelling, strong visual hierarchy, and guided interaction.

7. Interaction Over Time

The experience is designed for medium-length engagement, with the ability to revisit sections.

8. Time-Based Design

The segmented structure makes each section feel like a chapter, encouraging flexible exploration.

9. Media References

The experience references documentary film, museum exhibitions, and digital storytelling platforms.

10. Behaviour Cues

These references encourage slow, thoughtful interaction and close attention to detail.

11. Emotional Response

The experience creates curiosity and appreciation, encouraging reflection.

12. Frustration

Some interactions are not immediately obvious, making navigation slightly confusing.

13. Satisfaction

The seamless integration of visuals and scrolling creates a highly immersive experience.
